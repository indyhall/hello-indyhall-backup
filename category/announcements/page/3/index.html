A new announcements format, plus Pumpkin Carving, a Dance Show, and Show and Tell!

October 9, 2015 Sam Abrams Leave a comment

Hi Hallers!

You’ll probably notice that this email looks different from how my announcements emails have looked in the last year… that is because I’m trying something new! After 53 announcements posts, it’s time to mix things up.

One change is that all of the great info about what is happening around the Hall will now be in my emails, so you can find it right away!

I’m also trying a new format for the video called What Are you Jammin On?*  I will talk to members about what is making you happy lately- whether it’s a project, an song or book you can’t get out of your head, or a new hobby you think about constantly… I want to hear about it! If you want to be on a future episode of What are you Jammin’ On? come talk to me!

So without further ado, here’s the first episode!

#day-to-day highlights!

Weds

Check out the Designs of Change show at ImpactHub, where you can see all of Next Step: Democracy’s signs in one place. You’ll even see some familiar work from Indy Hall Arts regulars Kelsey, Sean, Mike Jackson, and more! 6-9pm

Thurs

It’s Show and Tell! Join us at noon in the classroom to hear from…

  • Matt Calhoun about the number 137 and the fine structure constant
  • Anna Goldfarb and what she’s been writing
  • Dean Rosenblum and the invention he’s been working on

Also, let’s carve some pumpkins! Join Kate, Adam, and more members for a night of pumpkin carving and prizes for the best designs. Bring your own pumpkin and your favorite fall drink (boozy or otherwise).

Saturday

Catch Savannah’s next dance show at l’Etage at 7pm- tickets are $15, and Sav will be dancing to Beyonce!

October 23rd

Red Sofa Salon turns 2!

What’s Red Sofa Salon, you ask? Created by awesome member Hila Ratzabi, the Red Sofa Salon is a series of poetry salons and writing workshops, creating an environment with “comfort, safety, and community, a place where we can share our writing in a respectful environment.” Doesn’t that sound pretty great?

For two years, Hila has held quarterly salons in Indy Hall’s gallery, and this Friday it’s time to celebrate that milestone! There will be poetry, of course, as well as cupcakes, drinks, and a dance party!

Grab your tickets here- $7 in advance, $10 at the door!

Let’s have a pre-Pope Happy Hour and Show!

I know, the Pope is coming this week. I’ll get to that in a minute. 

First! For the first time ever, Untitled, one of my improv groups, is coming to Indy Hall to do a show! Plus, beforehand, we’ll have a happy hour, because with all the transportation silliness, we’ll need a drink with friends.

So what does that mean for you? If you want to join us, and I hope you do, you can come grab a drink with your Indy Hall friends at 6pm down in the gallery, and at 7pm we’ll start the show!

My improv group and I will talk to a few of the artists about the work in KIN, and we’ll perform a 45-minute show completely made up on the spot. You’ll laugh, you’ll have a great time, and no one will make you go onstage (don’t worry)! If you’ve ever said, “yeah, I’ve wanted to see Sam do improv but not have to find where she performs,” you’re in luck! All of the details are in this post, or you can check the meetup!

Popeapocalypse is nigh!

You excited? You stressed? Somewhere in between? We’re here for you.

Indy Hall will be OPEN all week.That’s right- if you can get here, we’re here.

Here’s what is important to know….

  • We want to make SURE that the doors get locked at night, especially near the end of the week. All these people are going to need a bathroom, and we don’t need it to be here.
  • There’s no trash pickup this week. If you were yearning for helping with the trash this week, I’m sorry. What you can do is make sure to break down your boxes, and be ready to give us a hand next week!
  • We love you. It’s going to be fine.

Indyskillz is killing it!

If you haven’t checked out Indyskillz in a while, you’re missing some great skill sharing going on! Check out Lydia’s post about what everyone has learned from each other this month, and hop on the Groupbuzz post or the #indyskillz channel in slack to see what you can learn or teach today!

Not sure what I’m talking about? Indyskillz is Indy Hall’s skill swap- how it works is you share three things you’d like to teach, and three things you’d like to learn, and members let you know if they want to teach or learn those things with you! Everything goes on the website, so you can look up who can teach you, or who might want to learn from you.

This is also put together by awesome remote member Lydia, so if you learn something cool, make sure to shoot her a message about it (and a picture, if possible!)- you’ll end up on next month’s roundup!

August 28, 2015 Sam Abrams Leave a comment

The fall season for Indy Hall Arts starts this Friday! 

Find out what’s coming up this week!

Don’t miss the notes in the post for LOTS of great stuff coming up in early September!

Plus, see if you can find the spot where imovie titles outsmart me 😉

#highlight-reel

KIN is coming!

September is coming, and you know what that means- the Indy Hall Arts Fall Season is beginning! It’s a stacked season, and it begins this Friday in the gallery at 5pm.

Join us for the opening of KIN, a collaborative exhibition featuring the always-growing Indy Hall community. You’ll see art by both well-established artists around the Hall, as well as artists showing work for the very first time. On a personal note, it’s also the first time I’m ever showing my work (and I know that’s the same story for most of our artists)!

I know all of our art openings are fun, because Sean masterfully combines the best things- music, art, beer, and the best people around (you!), but this show moves beyond that.

This show puts together work from 25 Indy Hall artists, all working together to create something amazing. The work all together show the influence this community has had on each of the artists, and it’s truly amazing. Don’t miss it.

The Future of the Internet!

Remember Rob Epler‘s amazing Show and Tell about the future of the internet?

Well it sparked so much interest and discussion that we are bringing it back this Wednesday at 12:15 in the classroom. Come by for some presentation, some discussion, and possibly some Terminator references? We’ll see.

#In case you missed it…

#fringe!

Fringe festival starts this Friday!

If, like me, you usually miss Fringe, or realize too late you’re itching to see a particular show, this year you have an awesome tool to use… the #fringe slack channel!

Hop on it to talk about shows you’re excited about, coordinate seeing shows together, and give the rest of us your thoughts on what you’ve seen!

The Afflictions Audiobook has arrived!

If you’ve talked to Amanda at all in the last few months, you know that this was a huge undertaking… and now it’s finally arrived!

You can snag chapter 1 in the presale, and the rest of the book launches September 15th! 

Want Your Little Baby’s Ice Cream Delivered to Indy Hall?

Last year, Little Baby’s did a Community-Supported Ice Cream Program, and it was DELICIOUS. 

This year, they want to make Indy Hall a dropoff point again!

Here’s how it works- you sign up and pay a fee (last year it averaged to saving a dollar or two per pint), and for three months you get two pints of experimental, exotic ice cream flavors delivered to the Hall, just for you! There are even vegan and gluten free options.

I’ll be getting a clearer headcount this week, so respond on the Groupbuzz post if you want to sign up!

State of the Hall is back!

Did you miss it?

State of the Hall is an opportunity to talk about how things are going at the hall, what works, what doesn’t, and what we can do to improve those things you don’t love.

This week, we’re taking inspiration from our fantastic New Members Lunch and opening up conversation about all the things at Indy Hall that seem to be generally understood, but aren’t clear to you. There is a lot around here that is unwritten, and if any of it doesn’t make sense to you, we want to talk about it.

Those of us that work to run Indy Hall only get to have that “admin/staff” perspective- we don’t get to see our community like you do- so we need your help! We want to know what you think!

Join us this Friday, 8/21 at 2pm in the classroom!
