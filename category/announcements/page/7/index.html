Our Winter Adventure has Dates & Prices!

January 17, 2015 Alex Hillman Leave a comment

The response for the first ever Indy Hall Winter Adventure have been great! We had over 26 people sign up with us as interested.

Before I outline the prices and other details that we’ve calculated, I want to give a HUUUUUUGE thank you to Kate Field for making tons of phone calls to different lodges, vehicle rental companies, and more really doing the hard work to get the details organized so I could figure out the final price. This wouldn’t be happening without Kate, period. <3 <3 <3

Dates and Times – March 13-15th

It was a close call with everyone’s availability and we also had to take the room costs into consideration. March 13-15th ended up being the best option. 

  • Departure Time: plan to be AT Indy Hall with your gear by 11am on Friday March 13th so we can get the vans loaded and be on the road by noon. The drive is a little over 6 hours, and leaving earlier will help us beat Friday traffic.
  • Return Time: we’ll plan to be back in Philadelphia between 11pm-12 midnight on Sunday March 15th, which means we’ll need leave the lodge between 4 and 5pm.

How we’ll get there

The best option for a group our size was to reserve two 15 passenger vans. Including estimated fuel costs, we were able to get FULL roundtrip transportation costs to $100 per person so long as we get at least 20 people registered. So, in the prices you see listed below, round trip transportation costs are all included!

Having 2 vans will give us room for additional gear, and we could realistically get up to 25 people with the 2 vans before needing to add a third.

Note: If we end up with less than 20 people, transportation costs may go up a bit. 

The only downside to this option (versus a coach with a driver, which was MUCH more expensive unless our group was 55 people) is that we will need to drive. If each leg of the trip is broken into two 3-hour driving “shifts”, it should be easy. In the registration form below, please note if you have a drivers license and are comfortable driving one of the vans for a 3 hour shift.

Our Lodge! High Peak Resort

http://www.highpeaksresort.com

We chose this option because it had the best rates and availability…and had some great bonuses that other options didn’t have like hot tubs. There’s also a free shuttle daily to Whiteface for activities.

Travel + Lodge prices are listed below.

Choose Your Own Adventure

Over half of the people who said they wanted to go said that they DIDN’T have plans to ski or snowboard. Which is great, and hopefully helps more people who don’t love winter sports to consider coming along!

So we decided to separate the trip into two parts: a price for travel/accommodations – which Indy Hall is organizing and collecting money for – and the activities, which you’ll be able to purchase tickets for yourself separately. 

Optional activity prices are listed below, but you are responsible for buying your own activity tickets. They’re all able to be bought online.

Lodging + Travel Options

All of these prices include taxes and fees.

  • $361 – 1 person solo queen bed. Includes travel. (1 shared room, your own bed). Max 2 people per room at this rate per person.
  • $257 – 1 person shared queen bed. Includes travel. (1 shared room, share bed). Max 4 people per room at this rate per person.
  • $722 – Couples, families, etc. Base 2 people sharing 1 room. Includes travel for both people. (1 room, 2 queen beds.) Additional people up to 4 total at $120/person covers sharing costs + travel. 

We’ve held 10 rooms at these rates. We’ll keep track of them as they fill up. If you see an option/configuration we haven’t thought of with this, let us know!

Note: unfortunately, we won’t be able to do refunds so you will be responsible for filling/selling your spot if you’re unable to go after purchasing. Sorry!

Indoor and non-sports Activites

The resort has indoor activities on-site, including spa treatments (priced separately) and relaxing in the hot tub (free!). The hotel bar and lobby have fireplaces to lounge by. We can bring games to play, too.

The lodge is right in the heart of Lake Placid’s “downtown” so there’s also shopping and restaurants within walking distance.

Outdoor Activities & Prices

These activities are totally optional, and the prices are based on what’s available on Whiteface’s website right now. You are responsible for booking your own activities, and prices are better when you book ahead of time. 

All of these options, and more can be found on Whiteface Mountain’s website: http://www.whiteface.com.

Save 25% on lift tickets buying here ahead of time (Thanks Phil Ives!): http://www.liftopia.com/results.php?RegionCatId=R_6&StartDate=03%2F13%2F2015&NumberOfDays=3

Some of the things you can do, and prices:

  • Saturday Whiteface Lift Ticket – $89
  • Sunday Whiteface Lift Ticket (Special discount price on Sunday ticket if purchased online in advance) – $48
  • 2 day whiteface lift ticket (combine both above) – $137
  • Olympic Bobsled Experience (~2 hours) – $90
  • Olympic Skeleton (~2 hours) – $75
  • Olympic Bobsled + Skeleton Experience Combo – $132
  • 2 hour ski/snowboard lessons, every day at 10am or 2pm (includes 1 day lift ticket & rental) – $129
  • 2 hour lesson only (no lift ticket/rental) – $46

Save your spot now! Deadline is January 30th.

Indy Hall’s First Winter Adventure

January 7, 2015 Alex Hillman Leave a comment

After the success of the first Indy Hall Beach House back in August and September, we’ve had some discussion about what our next adventure should be. And given the snowy season – and the number of Indy Hall members that are into winter sports – we thought that it’d be fun to take a weekend trip to upstate New York!

At this point we’re talking to a few places to figure out prices for everything from accommodations to renting a mini-bus or coach so we don’t have carpool hell + worrying about who gets stuck driving after 2 long days on the mountain.

While we’re figuring out the nitty gritty and the pricing, we wanted to get an idea of what the interested headcount would be. This is important because the more people we get, the better pricing we can get.

Here’s the all-important details that we have so far:

We’re looking at dates at the end of February or beginning of March. On the table are the weekends of:

  • Feb 27th-Mar 1st
  • Mar 13th-15th
  • Mar 20th-22nd

In all cases, we’d plan to leave Philadelphia mid-afternoon on Friday to arrive Friday night, have all day Saturday and Sunday on the mountain, and then leave to return to Philly Sunday late afternoon.

Below is a form that asks for a bit of info from you, and that you’d select which dates COULD work for you. We’ll end up picking the most popular dates and ruling out the ones that don’t work for most people.

Whiteface Mountain has some of the best options in terms of price and availability for us, so that’s where we’re focusing our efforts.

Don’t ski or snowboard? YOU CAN STILL COME!

Whiteface has other activities, including snowtubing and other…less athletic fun things to do in the winter. That includes my personal favorite, which is sitting by the fire and sipping a warm boozy drink 🙂

Prices – when we have them – will be based on one person per bed, as well as some couples options like sharing a queen or king. If you’re looking to save money and don’t mind sharing a queen or full sized bed, just mention it in the notes when you submit the form below.

MOST IMPORTANTLY we need to have this form filled out by Tuesday, January 13th. Submitting this form DOES NOT commit you to the trip, but be aware that as soon as possible we will be collecting deposits to finalize everything.

Any questions or ideas, don’t hesitate to reach out to [email protected], or jump into the #snowtrip room on Slack 🙂

Got resolutions?

Some years, it is like the Resolution-ary War* trying to keep them, am I right? The only times that New Years Resolutions have worked for me is when I get other people involved– even just having someone else know what I’m working on keeps me accountable and makes me feel supported. 🙂

Indy Hall has a few resources to share your resolutions with the community!

The first is the #goals channel on slack. This woefully underused channel is perfect for posting daily goals, or what you’re doing to work on larger goals. Alex, Adam, and I have been doing this daily, and it’s been an awesome way to keep each other on track and get invested in each other’s goals.

The second resource is #indyskillz, Indy Hall’s skill swap.  You post three things you would like to learn, and three things you can teach, and hopefully you contact someone, or someone contacts you to swap!

There are a few places to post- the #indyskillz slack channel, one of the Groupbuzz posts summarizing the past month’s successes, or on the indyskillz archive on hello.indyhall.org.

 

(this is what it looks like on the page).

I’ve learned a few things from Indyskillz, I highly recommend checking it out! The archive would also be a great way to see if anyone shares your interests, or could teach you something to jump start your resolution!

  2.  Are you full of craft? Come knit, crochet, embroider, draw, or whatever else you’d like to do to unwind! We’ll meet at 2pm at the upstairs couches for about an hour. Want to learn one of these skills? Come on by!

Thursday

  1. It’s Show and Tell!!! come to the classroom area at noon to learn about some awesome topics!
    1. Thomas Thomas will be teaching us some chess basics, and how to watch the world chess championship
    2. Ellen Chenoweth will teach us about modern dance
    3. Kotaro Fujita will teach us “How to make a conference, JFDI style”
  2. Interested in writing right? Indy Hall Writer’s Group will be meeting at 12:30 (or after Show and Tell, whichever comes first) at the larger tables upstairs. Talk to Jacki or Erika for more information!
  3. Do you listen to Serial? Kate and other Serial fans are gathering at 12:30 in the classroom area to listen to the 11th episode of Serial, and to discuss who we think is guilty or innocent.
  4. Shhhh! Meditation time is at 1:30 in the North conference room.
  5. It’s an Indy Hall-I-Day Cookie Swap! Bring a bunch of cookies or another delicious treat to swap and share! Swapping will start at 6pm- come hang out and make some new sweet-toothed friends! Wild cookie monsters couldn’t keep me away!
  6. Night Owls, 6-10(ish) pm, will be hosted by lovely Laura! Eric Steele will also be holding Creative Night Owls, where folks will gather in the classroom to work on those creative side projects we’ve been saying we will totally get to eventually.

Friday

Bad Kitten at the Brewery! Come see Neil and I (and the rest of our fantastic improv group) compete in the Brew Ha Ha, a cage match style improv competition. We are taking on reigning champions RobProv, who beat us back in October with a controversial tie-breaker. Tickets are $5 at the door, and it’s BYOB.

 

 

What’s that? You wanted to hear more about Neil’s and my comedy endeavors? Well! Come see Two Late, a late night show we work on, akin to Jimmy Fallon or Conan, at Philly Improv Theater at midnight. This month’s guests are radio legend Pierre Robert and local puppeteer Joe Sabatino. Buy your tickets here, and use your discount code INDYTIX!

Saturday

Bring your masks, your kids, and your winter holiday spirit to Krampuslauf! Meeting in Liberty Lands at 4:30-6pm, it’ll involve a parade, fire juggling, and pictures with Krampus himself, Ben Levin!
